# Product Playbook

This document captures what stays consistent across DISH marketing campaigns and what should change by product.

The goal is to make the agent system reusable across multiple hospitality offers without forcing every campaign into the same message or channel logic.

## What Stays Constant

These rules should apply across most DISH campaigns:

- audience is restaurant and hospitality operators
- messaging should stay practical, commercially grounded, and operationally relevant
- human review is required before public launch
- unsupported savings, growth, or performance claims should never be introduced
- DE campaigns should preserve explicit tone guidance such as `Sie` or `Du`
- agents should favor hospitality-specific pains over generic technology language

## Shared Campaign Structure

Each DISH campaign should usually include:

1. a product-specific sample campaign file in `knowledge/`
2. a CMO output
3. a research output
4. a messaging output
5. a filled campaign brief
6. copy outputs for landing page, email, and paid
7. an analytics and optimization plan

## What Changes By Product

The system should adapt these variables per DISH product:

- primary pain point
- commercial trigger
- operator workflow context
- strongest proof points
- likely objections
- best opening message angle
- best-fit channels or creative emphasis

## DISH Order

### Core Pain

- high aggregator commissions
- weak ownership of the guest relationship
- need for a direct digital ordering path

### Main Value Story

- more direct online orders
- fewer per-order commissions
- stronger restaurant brand ownership

### Strongest Proof Themes

- no commission per order
- direct payment
- own-branded webshop
- mobile-optimized ordering flow

### Key Objections

- setup effort
- guest adoption
- switching away from aggregator habits

### Recommended Message Angles

- fewer commissions, more direct orders
- own the order, own the guest relationship
- direct ordering that fits restaurant operations

## DISH Reservation

### Core Pain

- booking commissions
- platform dependency
- manual reservation coordination
- concern about no-shows

### Main Value Story

- more direct table reservations
- no booking commission
- simpler guest booking experience

### Strongest Proof Themes

- commission-free online booking
- 24/7 reservation availability
- automated reminders
- Google Maps integration

### Key Objections

- guest adoption
- setup effort
- uncertainty about workflow change
- risk of overstating no-show benefits

### Recommended Message Angles

- fewer booking commissions, more direct reservations
- make reservations easier for guests and teams
- direct booking under your restaurant's control

## Cross-Product Messaging Rules

- lead with the clearest business pain first
- support the economic story with operational simplicity
- avoid abstract digital-transformation language
- keep product promises tightly tied to approved features
- do not make one product sound interchangeable with another

## Reusable Output Checklist

Before starting a new product campaign, confirm:

- the sample campaign names the correct DISH product
- proof themes match `knowledge/offers.md`
- objections are product-specific
- market and language are explicit
- campaign angles fit the actual operator pain
- analytics plan tracks the right primary KPI

## Recommended Next Product

`DISH POS` is the next best test because it introduces a different operational and compliance story:

- checkout flow
- fiscalization and compliance
- speed and reliability at point of sale

That makes it a strong test of whether the agent system can handle products that are less about direct digital demand and more about in-venue operations.

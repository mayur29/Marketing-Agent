# Market Research Output

## Campaign

DISH Order DE Launch Push

## Research Focus

Understand what makes German restaurant operators consider shifting more order volume away from aggregator-led ordering toward a direct branded ordering channel.

## Market and Language

- market: Germany
- primary language: German
- tone register: DE `Sie`

## Key Findings

- aggregator commissions and limited ownership of the guest relationship are strong commercial pain points
- operators are likely to respond well to transparent pricing and practical operational simplicity
- setup effort, menu onboarding, and fear of low guest adoption are likely objections that should be addressed directly
- messaging should balance margin improvement with ease of implementation rather than relying only on abstract digital-transformation language

## Evidence and Signals

- DISH Order value proposition centers on no commission per order and direct branded ordering
- the approved offer context emphasizes mobile optimization and direct payment
- the campaign audience is price-sensitive and operationally constrained, which makes ease and clarity important alongside economics

## Strategic Implications

- lead with direct-order economics, but support it with simple operational language
- avoid broad promises about growth unless supported by approved proof
- frame DISH Order as a practical business tool, not just a digital brand upgrade
- include reassurance around implementation effort and day-to-day usability

## Confidence Level

Medium

## Recommended Knowledge Updates

- add recurring objections around onboarding, menu setup, and guest adoption to audience knowledge
- add a note that German operator messaging should emphasize transparency, trust, and practical implementation
- add a competitor-analysis section in future iterations covering aggregator alternatives and direct-order competitors

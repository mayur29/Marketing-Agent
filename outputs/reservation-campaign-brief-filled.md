# Campaign Brief

## Campaign Name

DISH Reservation DE Demand Push

## Objective

- Generate qualified demo requests for DISH Reservation from German restaurant operators.

## Target Audience

- Restaurants that depend on reservations for dine-in revenue
- Operators frustrated by third-party booking commissions
- Hospitality businesses that want a simpler booking experience and fewer no-shows

## Market and Language

- Country or market: Germany
- Primary language: German
- Tone register: DE `Sie`

## Offer

- DISH Reservation, a commission-free online table booking tool with 24/7 booking, automated reminders, and Google Maps integration

## Core Message

- Give guests a direct and simple way to reserve tables online
- Reduce dependence on commission-based booking platforms
- Support smoother reservation workflows for your restaurant team

## Supporting Proof

- commission-free online table booking
- 24/7 reservation availability
- automated reminders
- Google Maps integration

## Funnel Stage

- Consideration to conversion

## Channels

- Landing page
- Email
- Meta ads
- Sales follow-up

## Assets Needed

- German landing page
- 3-email German nurture or outbound sequence
- Meta ad copy variants
- Sales follow-up summary sheet

## CTA

- Demo buchen

## KPIs

- Primary KPI: qualified demo requests
- Secondary KPIs: landing page conversion rate, email click-through rate, cost per qualified lead
- Leading indicators: hero-to-CTA conversion, form completion rate, ad click-through rate

## Timeline

- Start date: to be defined
- Major milestones: messaging approval, asset draft review, launch, first performance review
- Launch date: to be defined
- Review checkpoints: pre-launch approval, week 1 review, week 2 optimization review

## Risks and Constraints

- do not use unsupported booking-growth or no-show-reduction claims
- operators may hesitate due to setup or guest-adoption concerns
- messaging must stay practical and credible

## Experiments

- test commission-free angle versus guest-convenience angle
- test direct-booking-control angle versus smoother-operations angle

## Approvals Required

- human review of final public-facing copy
- human review of any booking-volume, no-show, or performance-sensitive claims
- human approval before campaign launch

## Notes

- message package should address setup effort and practical workflow fit
- German tone should remain formal and trustworthy

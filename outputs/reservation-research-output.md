# Market Research Output

## Campaign

DISH Reservation DE Demand Push

## Research Focus

Understand what makes German restaurant operators consider shifting more reservation activity toward a direct booking tool instead of relying on commission-based platforms or manual booking processes.

## Market and Language

- market: Germany
- primary language: German
- tone register: DE `Sie`

## Key Findings

- commission-heavy booking channels reduce perceived profitability of reservations
- operators value a simpler guest booking path that does not add front-of-house coordination overhead
- no-shows and fragmented reservation management are likely meaningful pain points
- booking tools should be positioned as practical, guest-friendly, and operationally supportive rather than overly technical

## Evidence and Signals

- DISH Reservation is defined as commission-free and available 24/7
- automated reminders and Google Maps integration support a convenience-and-operations story
- the target audience is likely to balance profitability with ease of guest adoption

## Strategic Implications

- lead with direct, commission-free booking economics
- support the value story with guest convenience and reduced coordination effort
- address no-show prevention carefully and only within approved proof boundaries
- position the offer as a practical reservation workflow improvement for daily restaurant operations

## Confidence Level

Medium

## Recommended Knowledge Updates

- add reservation-specific objections around guest adoption, integration effort, and no-show expectations
- add a note that reservation messaging should balance economics with smoother guest booking flow
- add competitor analysis for reservation platforms in Germany in a future iteration

# Market Research Output

## Campaign

DISH POS DE Conversion Push

## Research Focus

Understand what makes German restaurant operators consider switching to a more reliable hospitality POS system and which operational pains matter most in the decision.

## Market and Language

- market: Germany
- primary language: German
- tone register: DE `Sie`

## Key Findings

- slow checkout and fragmented workflows create daily operational frustration
- compliance requirements add perceived risk and complexity in Germany
- operators are likely to respond to a practical combination of speed, reliability, and compliance support
- technical feature language should be translated into service flow and operational confidence

## Evidence and Signals

- DISH POS includes offline mode, inventory management, and German fiscalization support
- the target audience likely values day-to-day reliability as much as system breadth
- POS decisions are tied closely to service flow, staff efficiency, and operational trust

## Strategic Implications

- lead with restaurant operations value first, not software architecture
- support the compliance story with reassurance rather than heavy jargon
- frame DISH POS as an all-in-one hospitality workflow, not just a register replacement
- address migration and onboarding concerns early

## Confidence Level

Medium

## Recommended Knowledge Updates

- add POS-specific objections around migration effort, training, and system disruption
- add a note that German POS messaging should balance compliance reassurance with operational simplicity
- add POS competitor analysis in Germany in a future iteration

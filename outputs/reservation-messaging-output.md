# Messaging and Positioning Output

## Campaign

DISH Reservation DE Demand Push

## Market and Language

- market: Germany
- primary language: German
- tone register: DE `Sie`

## Positioning Statement

For restaurant operators in Germany who want to manage more reservations directly and reduce dependence on commission-based booking platforms, DISH Reservation provides a simple online booking experience under the restaurant's own control.

## Core Value Proposition

Take reservations directly, stay closer to your guests, and offer a simple booking experience without paying commission on each reservation.

## Key Messages

- reduce dependence on commission-based booking platforms
- give guests an easy way to reserve online at any time
- support smoother reservation workflows with automated reminders
- keep the booking experience closer to your own restaurant brand

## Proof Points

- commission-free online table booking
- 24/7 reservation availability
- automated reminders
- Google Maps integration

## Objections to Address

- concern that guests will continue using large booking platforms
- uncertainty about setup or adoption effort
- questions about how much day-to-day effort the booking workflow requires

## Tone Guidance

Practical, trustworthy, guest-aware, and operationally grounded. Avoid inflated booking-growth claims or overly abstract digital language.

## Recommended Campaign Angles

- fewer booking commissions, more direct reservations
- make reservations easier for guests and teams
- direct table booking under your restaurant's control

## What To Emphasize

- commission-free direct booking
- guest convenience
- operational simplicity
- stronger control of reservation flow

## What To Avoid

- unsupported booking-growth guarantees
- exaggerated no-show reduction claims
- technical jargon without restaurant relevance

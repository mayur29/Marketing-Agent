# Messaging and Positioning Output

## Campaign

DISH Order DE Launch Push

## Market and Language

- market: Germany
- primary language: German
- tone register: DE `Sie`

## Positioning Statement

For restaurant operators in Germany who want more direct online orders and better margin control, DISH Order provides an own-branded ordering experience without per-order commission fees.

## Core Value Proposition

Keep more of each order, strengthen the direct guest relationship, and offer customers a simple branded way to order directly from your restaurant.

## Key Messages

- reduce dependence on commission-heavy delivery platforms
- create a direct ordering path under your own brand
- offer guests a mobile-friendly experience for pickup and delivery
- support restaurant economics with transparent, practical digital ordering

## Proof Points

- no commission per order
- direct payment through the restaurant's own branded experience
- mobile-optimized ordering flow
- stronger ownership of the guest relationship

## Objections to Address

- concern that setup and menu onboarding will take too much time
- doubt that guests will switch from aggregator apps
- uncertainty about operational effort for staff

## Tone Guidance

Practical, trustworthy, commercially grounded, and operator-aware. Avoid hype, inflated ROI language, or abstract innovation language.

## Recommended Campaign Angles

- fewer commissions, more direct orders
- own the order, own the guest relationship
- direct ordering that fits everyday restaurant operations

## What To Emphasize

- margin protection
- transparency
- operational practicality
- guest relationship ownership

## What To Avoid

- unsupported revenue guarantees
- vague innovation language
- claims that imply zero setup effort
- aggressive anti-aggregator language without proof

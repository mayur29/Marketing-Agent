# Campaign Brief

## Campaign Name

DISH Order DE Launch Push

## Objective

- Generate qualified demo requests for DISH Order from German restaurant operators.

## Target Audience

- Independent restaurant owners in Germany
- Small hospitality groups with delivery or pickup demand
- Operators frustrated by aggregator commissions and weak direct customer ownership

## Market and Language

- Country or market: Germany
- Primary language: German
- Tone register: DE `Sie`

## Offer

- DISH Order, an own-branded webshop for delivery and pickup with direct payment and no commission per order

## Core Message

- Give your restaurant a direct ordering channel under your own brand
- Reduce dependence on commission-heavy platforms
- Make it easier for guests to order directly for pickup or delivery

## Supporting Proof

- no commission per order
- direct payment through the restaurant's own branded experience
- mobile-optimized ordering flow
- stronger ownership of the guest relationship

## Funnel Stage

- Consideration to conversion

## Channels

- Landing page
- Email
- Meta ads
- Sales follow-up

## Assets Needed

- German landing page
- 3-email German nurture or outbound sequence
- Meta ad copy variants
- Sales follow-up summary sheet

## CTA

- Demo buchen

## KPIs

- Primary KPI: qualified demo requests
- Secondary KPIs: landing page conversion rate, email click-through rate, cost per qualified lead
- Leading indicators: hero-to-CTA conversion, form completion rate, ad click-through rate

## Timeline

- Start date: to be defined
- Major milestones: messaging approval, asset draft review, launch, first performance review
- Launch date: to be defined
- Review checkpoints: pre-launch approval, week 1 review, week 2 optimization review

## Risks and Constraints

- do not use unsupported savings or revenue claims
- operators may hesitate due to setup or adoption concerns
- messaging must stay practical and credible

## Experiments

- test commission-savings angle versus guest-relationship angle
- test direct-order convenience angle versus brand-control angle

## Approvals Required

- human review of final public-facing copy
- human review of any pricing, savings, or performance-sensitive claims
- human approval before campaign launch

## Notes

- message package should address setup effort and practical day-to-day usage
- German tone should remain formal and trustworthy

- Draft

- Expected output: landing page hero draft, email sequence draft, and paid copy variants
- Preserve context: Germany, German language, DE `Sie`, practical hospitality tone, no unsupported savings claims
